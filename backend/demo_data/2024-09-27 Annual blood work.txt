September 27, 2024

Had my big annual blood draw this morning. Seven vials! Dr. Martinez ordered a CBC, metabolic panel, and thyroid check all at once since I've been dragging lately. Fasting too, which meant no coffee until after — that was brutal.

Got the results back this afternoon and there's a lot to unpack.

First, the anemia thing is real. My hemoglobin is 11.2 (low end of normal is 12.0) and hematocrit is 34.1%. The RDW is elevated too at 15.8%, which the lab flagged. The comment says it's consistent with iron deficiency and they're recommending iron studies. That would explain so much — the fatigue, the brain fog, feeling winded going up one flight of stairs. I've been chalking it up to stress and recovering from surgery but maybe it's been anemia the whole time.

The metabolic panel showed my glucose at 142 fasting, which is definitely in diabetes territory. No surprise there — I've been managing type 2 for a few years now. My kidney function looks good though (eGFR >90, creatinine 0.92), so that's a relief. Everything else was in range — electrolytes, liver enzymes, all that.

TSH was 2.41 which is smack in the middle of normal. So my thyroid isn't the culprit for the fatigue.

So the picture is: iron deficiency anemia + diabetes that needs tighter control. Dr. Martinez wants me to start iron supplements and recheck in a couple months. She also scheduled an A1c for November to see where things really stand.

I'm actually kind of relieved to have some concrete answers. Being tired all the time with no explanation was making me feel like I was just lazy or depressed. Now I know there's a physical reason and something I can do about it.
