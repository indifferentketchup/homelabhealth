December 12, 2024

First appointment with Dr. Patel, the allergist/immunologist that Dr. Martinez referred me to. I was nervous — seeing a specialist always feels like a step up in seriousness.

Dr. Patel is great though. Really listened, asked detailed questions. She was particularly interested in the facial swelling episodes. I described how my lips and cheeks puff up, sometimes my eyelids too, lasting anywhere from a few hours to a day. No hives, no itching — just swelling. She asked if I ever get abdominal pain with it and I realized yes, sometimes I do get a crampy stomach ache around the same time but I always chalked it up to something I ate.

She mentioned hereditary angioedema before I even brought it up. Apparently the pattern — recurrent swelling without hives, sometimes with abdominal pain, normal response to antihistamines — is classic for HAE. She was very careful to say she's not diagnosing me, just noting that the pattern fits.

She drew blood for two tests: C4 complement level and serum tryptase. The C4 is typically low in HAE patients because the complement system gets activated when it shouldn't be. The tryptase checks for mast cell disorders, which can cause similar swelling.

The tryptase came back normal today at 4.8 ng/mL (cutoff is 11.4). So probably not a mast cell issue, which is good because those are harder to treat. The C4 is low though — 12 mg/dL when the normal range starts at 14. That's flagged as low. Dr. Patel's note says "low C4 complement level — clinical correlation recommended."

She ordered two more tests: C1 esterase inhibitor antigen and functional assay. Those are the definitive tests for HAE. I have to wait a few days for those to come back.

I'm trying not to get ahead of myself but I've been reading about HAE and some things click. The abdominal pain episodes I've had over the years that were always "maybe IBS" or "maybe gallbladder" (before they found the stones). The facial swelling that nobody could explain. Even my mom used to get weird swelling episodes.

We might finally have an answer.
