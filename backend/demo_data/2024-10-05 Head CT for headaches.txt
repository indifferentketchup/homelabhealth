October 5, 2024

The headaches have been getting worse. Not like a normal headache — these feel like someone is squeezing the inside of my skull. They come with this weird pressure behind my eyes and sometimes my face feels puffy, especially around my lips and cheeks. Dr. Martinez got concerned enough to order a CT angiogram of my head to check the blood vessels. She wanted to rule out an aneurysm.

That word — aneurysm — really rattled me. I know it's probably just precautionary, but still. You don't want doctors looking for things like that.

The CTA itself was quick. They injected contrast through an IV and the machine spun around my head. The warm flush from the contrast dye is such a weird sensation — like you wet your pants but it's just the dye spreading through your body.

Results came back today. Everything is normal. All the arteries in my brain are patent, no narrowing, no aneurysm. The Circle of Willis is complete and symmetric (which apparently is a good thing — some people have anatomical variants that can cause problems). No bleeding, no clots, no fractures.

I almost cried with relief. Dr. Chen the neuroradiologist signed off on it personally. He said my brain vasculature looks "unremarkable," which in doctor-speak is exactly what you want to hear.

So the headaches aren't from anything vascular or structural. That's good news but also a little frustrating because we still don't know what's causing them. The facial swelling is still unexplained too. Dr. Martinez mentioned maybe seeing an allergist if things don't settle down.

For now I'm just going to sit with the relief. No aneurysm. No tumor. No blocked arteries. My brain is physically okay.
