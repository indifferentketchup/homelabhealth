November 17, 2024

Had the MRI today that Dr. Martinez ordered after the CTA came back normal for blood vessels. She wanted a more detailed look at the brain tissue itself since the headaches aren't letting up and now there's this weird facial swelling that comes and goes.

The MRI was long — almost an hour in that loud tube. They did scans without contrast first, then injected gadolinium and did another set. The contrast makes everything feel cold in your veins and you get a metallic taste for a few seconds. The tech gave me headphones playing classical music which helped a little but you can still hear the machine banging and whirring through it.

Results are in. No tumor, no mass, no bleeding — thank god. No evidence of multiple sclerosis either which I was low-key worried about. But there is something: scattered little spots of brightness on the T2/FLAIR images in the white matter of my brain. The report says they're "nonspecific" and there aren't very many of them.

Dr. Chen the neuroradiologist offered two possible explanations: early small vessel disease (the kind of changes you see with chronic high blood pressure or diabetes) or migraine-related changes. Given that I'm only 34, the small vessel disease explanation would be "atypical for patient age" — his words. He leans more toward migraine as the explanation, especially since I've been having these headaches.

They also found mild mucosal thickening in my maxillary sinuses, which might explain some of the facial pressure. Nothing serious.

The facial swelling episodes are still a mystery. Dr. Martinez has been great about connecting dots — she suggested the allergist idea based on the swelling pattern, not the MRI findings. The MRI doesn't explain that part at all.

One weird thing: when I look up "facial swelling episodes + normal brain MRI + recurrent headaches," hereditary angioedema keeps coming up. I've never heard of that before. I'm not going to go down a Google rabbit hole, but I'll mention it to the allergist when I see them.
