September 10, 2024

I had my follow-up ultrasound today to check how everything is healing after the gallbladder surgery last month. I've been feeling pretty good — the sharp upper-right pain that used to hit after meals is completely gone now. Still a little sore around the incisions but nothing major.

The ultrasound tech was really nice. She spent a lot of time looking at my liver and the area where my gallbladder used to be. She said everything looked normal from what she could see, though obviously she can't give official results.

Dr. Nguyen called with the official report this afternoon. Everything came back normal — liver looks good at 14.2 cm with no lesions, the bile duct is measuring 4 mm which is totally fine for someone post-surgery. Kidneys, spleen, pancreas all unremarkable. No fluid buildup anywhere.

Honestly I was a little nervous going in. You hear stories about complications after gallbladder surgery — bile leaks, leftover stones, that kind of thing. But it looks like I'm in the clear. No more attacks, no more midnight trips to the ER wondering if I'm dying. That alone makes the surgery worth it.

She did note my common bile duct is on the smaller side at 4 mm but said that's completely normal after cholecystectomy — up to 10 mm is fine in post-op patients. It's wild how the body adapts.

So that's one thing off the health checklist. Now to deal with this fatigue that won't quit and those headaches that keep coming back. But one win at a time.
