November 15, 2024

A1c and cholesterol check today. I've been on the iron supplements for almost two months now and I do feel a little better — less winded, more energy in the afternoons. Still not great but improving.

The A1c came back at 7.2%. That's actually down from 7.5% in May and way down from 8.1% last year. The trend is going in the right direction! The estimated average glucose is 160 which is still high but the trajectory matters more than any single number. ADA says under 7.0% is the goal for most adults so I'm almost there.

The lipid panel wasn't as encouraging. Total cholesterol 218 (should be under 200), LDL 143 (optimal is under 100), triglycerides 165 (should be under 150). My HDL is 42 which is borderline low. The ratio is 5.2 and they want it under 5.0.

Dr. Martinez's notes mention considering a statin given that I have both diabetes and what she calls "likely hypertension." I've been checking my blood pressure at home and it does run a little high — usually around 135/85 or so. She said diabetes plus high cholesterol plus borderline hypertension is the trifecta they worry about for heart disease.

The idea of adding another daily pill to my routine isn't appealing, but I also don't want to be 55 with a heart attack. I'll talk to her about it at the next visit.

On the bright side, I've been tracking my meals better and walking after dinner most nights. Little changes adding up. The A1c improvement proves I can move the needle.
