January 10, 2025

New year, new treatment plan. Dr. Patel prescribed lanadelumab injections — it's a monoclonal antibody that blocks kallikrein, which is the enzyme that goes into overdrive when you have HAE. Every two weeks, subcutaneous injection. I was really nervous about giving myself shots but the auto-injector makes it almost foolproof.

Did my first injection today in the office with a nurse watching. It stings for about 10 seconds and then it's over. Way easier than I expected. The nurse said most people get used to it within a few weeks.

Dr. Patel switched my blood pressure medication from lisinopril to amlodipine. She said ACE inhibitors are absolutely contraindicated with HAE — they can trigger attacks by increasing bradykinin levels. I'm honestly a little frustrated that nobody caught this before. I've been on lisinopril for two years, and all of my worst swelling episodes happened during that time. Would I have had fewer attacks without it? No way to know, but it's hard not to wonder.

The iron supplements are finally working — my last CBC showed hemoglobin at 13.1 and hematocrit at 38%. Almost normal. The fatigue has improved a lot. I have actual energy in the evenings now instead of collapsing on the couch after work.

My A1c was 6.9% on the home meter this morning. Under 7! First time in years. The evening walks and cutting back on sugary drinks are paying off. Dr. Martinez will be happy.

I feel cautiously optimistic for the first time in a while. Having a name for the HAE, having a treatment plan, seeing my numbers improve — it feels like I'm finally getting ahead of things instead of just reacting to whatever my body throws at me.

Next appointment with Dr. Patel is in March to see how the lanadelumab is working. She said most patients see a dramatic reduction in attacks within the first month. I hope I'm one of them.
