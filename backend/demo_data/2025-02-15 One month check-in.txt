February 15, 2025

Six weeks on lanadelumab and I can honestly say my life has changed. I've had two minor swelling episodes in that time — both just some lip puffiness that resolved in a few hours. Before treatment I was having attacks every 7-10 days. The difference is night and day.

The genetic test results came back last week and confirmed HAE Type 1 — SERPING1 gene mutation, exactly as expected. Having the genetic proof feels validating. This is real, it has a code, it's not in my head.

I talked to my brother. It was a difficult conversation but he took it well. He's getting tested next month. I hope he doesn't have it, but if he does, at least he'll know before he goes through years of unexplained symptoms like I did.

My diabetes management is going well. A1c was 6.8% at my February checkup — holding steady under 7. My cholesterol is still a little high (total 205, LDL 135) but trending down. Dr. Martinez decided to hold off on the statin for now and see if diet and exercise can get me the rest of the way there. I'm okay with that — I'd rather avoid another medication if I can.

The iron levels are completely normal now. Ferritin was 68, iron saturation 28%. I feel like a different person compared to September when I could barely climb stairs.

I've been thinking about how much has changed in five months. Last fall I was exhausted, having unexplained swelling and headaches, my A1c was going in the wrong direction, and I had no idea what was wrong with me. Now I have a diagnosis, a treatment that works, and blood work that's mostly in range. It didn't happen overnight — it took good doctors, persistence, and a lot of blood draws — but we got here.

I'm going to keep journaling. It helps me see patterns and it helps my doctors understand what's happening between visits. Plus, reading back through these entries reminds me how far I've come.
